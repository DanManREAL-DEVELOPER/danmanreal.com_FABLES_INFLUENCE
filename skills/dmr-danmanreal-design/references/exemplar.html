Hard in the paint

Before the engines and the WebGPU worlds, every asset on this channel was drawn pixel-by-pixel in Microsoft Paint. The paint never dried. It runs down every frame on this site — street-wet, jewel-lit, bronze-framed — and this wall is its law. Colors are measured from the real hero GLBs, never invented.

IIThe Paint — liquid, never banner

Caught mid-drip, every time.

Paint on this site is alive: a tongue swells from the bar, necks, and a droplet tears free — real liquid behavior, built from a gaussian-blur + alpha-contrast goo filter fusing animated shapes into one wet body.

The Paint Labblue · street accents · green — one liquid law
Swell.

A tongue grows from the bar on a slow ease — paint gathering weight, never a static banner.

Neck.

The goo filter fuses drop and tongue while they touch; the join thins as they part — surface tension for free.

Tear.

Past the goo radius the droplet snaps free and falls, fading out before it ever hits layout.

Gloss.

A sharp highlight rides outside the filter so the wet edge stays crisp while the body stays gooey.

IIIThe Frame — the UI is a gallery

Everything ships in a frame.

No naked blocks. Every card, video, and panel hangs on the wall in bronze-veined gunmetal with jewel-lit corners and paint running off the top edge — the same frame family measured from the hero GLBs.

IVThe Voice — large or silent

Type goes big, and stays readable.

Display type is heavy and huge. Body copy never drops below a light mint-white — deep grey on the abyss is outlawed. If a line matters, it gets size; if it doesn't, it gets cut.

VIThe Law — how the wall is used

Do it wet, or don't do it.

ALWAYS

  • Measure color from the GLB assets — BRAND_TOKENS.css is the only palette.
  • Frame every block: gunmetal + bronze veins + jewel corners.
  • Keep paint liquid — goo-filtered, mid-drip, gloss on top.
  • Go LARGE with display type; keep body text mint-bright.
  • Give chapters air — one idea per frame, generous space between.
  • Blue leads, green answers; purple/pink only as street accents.

NEVER

  • Invent hex values or drift back to old CSS colors.
  • Ship a naked, frameless block — this UI is a gallery wall.
  • Draw paint as flat static banners or hard-edged bars.
  • Set body copy in deep grey on the abyss.
  • Cram sections together — crowding kills the wall.
  • Let decoration own the content — frames serve, media plays.
